A Walk Through the City of Arts and Sciences

  • Valencia is a vibrant city, full of history and tradition, yet at the same time modern and avant-garde. Located on the east coast of Spain, Valencia has a Mediterranean climate that invites you to enjoy the city all year round. It is the third largest city in Spain, and its port is one of the most important in the Mediterranean. The history of Valencia is ancient and rich. It was founded by the Romans in 138 BC and was later occupied by Visigoths, Arabs, and finally by Christians in the 13th century. Each of these peoples left their mark on the city’s architecture and culture. From the medieval towers to the modernist jewels and the bold contemporary design of the City of Arts and Sciences, Valencia is a city of contrasts where the old meets the new. An Oasis in the City Valencia boasts a privileged coastline with several golden sand beaches. The most famous is Malvarrosa Beach, which stretches along the city’s seafront promenade. Here you’ll find a lively restaurant and bar scene, as well as a host of water activities to enjoy. Just north of Malvarrosa is Patacona Beach. Less crowded but equally charming, Patacona offers a quieter alternative to the bustling Malvarrosa. It is especially known for its chiringuitos, small beach bars where you can enjoy a cold beer and tapas while watching the sunset over the Mediterranean. To the south of the city, you’ll find the beaches of Pinedo and El Saler, surrounded by the Albufera Natural Park. These beaches are quieter and offer a more natural environment. Moreover, El Saler is known for its golf course, considered one of the best in Spain. In any case, Valencia’s beaches are the perfect place to relax and soak up the Spanish sun.
